About CVEU

This workshop is for the 2nd installment of the AI for Creative Video Editing and Understanding (CVEU), which follows its succes on the first launch at ICCV2021.

The workshop brings together researchers, artists and entrepreneurs working on computer vision, machine learning, computer graphics, human computer interaction, and cognitive research.

It aims to bring awareness of recent advances in machine learning technologies to enable assisted creative-video creation and understanding.

Program

Discuss recent advances in creative video understanding, creation and editing. Some of the topics that we plan to discuss are:

  • Can AI reduce the human cost of video productions?
  • How to design intuitive video editing tools?
  • How do humans and AI collaborate together to inspire creativity?
  • Would AI kill video editing jobs?
  • Are there any biases and threats by using AI in this field?

    Computers, Creativity, and Lovelace
    In this talk we examine the different ways humans and AI systems can create together. While not strictly necessary, we may wish that AI creative partners themselves possess the quality of creativity. We look at what sort of attributes we should expect in human-level creative systems. I conclude the talk with some informal analysis of recent progress toward AI systems that express creativity.
    Reimagining Creative Video Editing Education
    Video creators always chase the latest technological innovations. Editors and supervisors consider mastering new workflows to be essential knowledge. But when it comes to learning storytelling skills, our industry grasps for a center of gravity. Is film school worth the investment? Do all editors need a mentor? Do YouTube tutorials hold the answers?
    Until recently, creative editing was an amorphous concept taught by people promising to "tell you everything they don't in film school." These instructors offer no measurable outcomes, no metrics to track progress or assess skill level.
    In this presentation, EditMentor CEO Misha Tenenbaum shares what he and his team believe is the future of learning creative video editing -- interactivity, gamification, and the use of data.
    Integration of Generative A.I. Models into Film Production
    Seyhan Lee has become the studio that bridges the worlds of cutting-edge ML research and the world of entertainment. This talk will showcase how we use generative models in the production pipeline, i.e., award-winning films, music videos, and immersive experiences. and the latest research from Seyhan Lee's creative lab. I will also give a glimpse of the tool we are developing to integrate generative A.I. into the Unreal Engine for virtual production.
    Lights, Camera, AI! – How Prime Video is Inventing AI technologies to Re-define the Production and Consumption of Long-form Video Content
    At Prime Video, we are always striving to create best-in-class video watching experiences for our customers. This requires us to develop novel AI technologies for video understanding and synthesis that can enable a variety of delightful video watching experiences in an efficient and scalable manner. A key challenge to this end is the large amounts of labelled training data required by the end-to-end supervised approaches that have traditionally been used for video understanding. In this talk, I will provide an overview of some of the self and weakly supervised approaches we have developed that can effectively address this challenge. These approaches can learn general purpose video representations in a label efficient manner and offer state-of-the-art performance for a wide variety of video understanding tasks. Similarly, in the space of video synthesis, the special effects workflows traditionally employed in the industry are highly manual and therefore not easily scalable. I will present an overview of some of the state-of-the-art methods we have developed to automate traditional special effects workflows, and will share results of these approaches on both cinematic as well as live-sports video content.

    Call for Papers

    Topics

    This workshop brings together researchers working on computer vision, machine listening, computer graphics, human-computer interaction, and cognitive research. It aims to bring awareness of recent advances in machine learning technologies to enable assisted creative-video creation and understanding. The workshop will include invited talks by experts in the area and give the community opportunities to share their work via oral and poster presentations. We encourage practitioners, designers, students, post-docs, and researchers to submit work describing new ideas, work-in-progress, and previously or concurrently published research. Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

    • Computational Video Editing
    • Computational Videography/Virtual Cinematography
    • Video Description and Understanding
    • Video Search and Retrieval
    • Video Shortening and Storyboarding
    • Video Manipulation
    • Event/Story Understanding
    • Emotion/Sound Recognition
    • Human-centric Video Understanding
    • Long-term Video Understanding
    • Generative Adversarial Network for Video
    • Neural Rendering for Video
    • Dataset and Evaluation


    Submission

    The CVEU workshop welcomes papers on the topics summarized above. We will publish articles in two tracks: 1) In-proceeding Track and and 2) Extended Abstract Track. All the accepted papers must be unpublished and original work related to topics of the CVEU workshop and have opportunities to be presented as orals or posters during the workshop.
