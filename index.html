ECCV 2022 Workshop on AI for Creative Video Editing and Understanding.

About CVEU

This workshop is for the 2nd installment of the AI for Creative Video Editing and Understanding (CVEU), which follows its succes on the first launch at ICCV2021.

The workshop brings together researchers, artists and entrepreneurs working on computer vision, machine learning, computer graphics, human computer interaction, and cognitive research.

It aims to bring awareness of recent advances in machine learning technologies to enable assisted creative-video creation and understanding.

Program

Discuss recent advances in video understanding in the realm of video creation and editing. Some of the topics that we plan to discuss are:

Can AI make humans reduce the cost of video productions?

How to design intuitive video editing tools?

Would AI kill video editing jobs?

Are there any biases and threads by using AI in this field?

Call for Papers

Topics

This workshop brings together researchers working on computer vision, machine listening, computer graphics, human-computer interaction, and cognitive research. It aims to bring awareness of recent advances in machine learning technologies to enable assisted creative-video creation and understanding. The workshop will include invited talks by experts in the area and give the community opportunities to share their work via oral and poster presentations. We encourage practitioners, designers, students, post-docs, and researchers to submit work describing new ideas, work-in-progress, and previously or concurrently published research. Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

  • Computational Video Editing
  • Computational Videography/Virtual Cinematography
  • Human-centric Video Understanding
  • Long-term Video Understanding
  • Generative Adversarial Network for Video
  • Emotion/Audio Recognition
  • Story/Event Understanding
  • Video Description and Understanding
  • Video Search and Retrieval
  • Video Shortening
  • Video Storyboarding
  • Video Manipulation


Submission

The CVEU workshop welcomes papers on the topics summarized above. We will publish articles in two tracks: 1) In-proceeding Track and and 2) Extended Abstract Track. All the accepted papers must be unpublished and original work related to topics of the CVEU workshop and have opportunities to be presented as orals or posters during the workshop.

In-proceeding Track

The papers submitted to this track are limited to 14 pages excluding references and will be included in the official ECCV workshop proceedings.



Extended Abstract Track

The articles submitted to this track will not be published in conjunction with ECCV 2022 proceedings. So that you could submit it to other conferences or journals. The papers are limited to 4 pages, including figures and tables, formatted according to the ECCV style. Additional pages containing only cited references are allowed.



Guidelines

Please use the ECCV template. All the submitted papers will go through a double-blind peer-review process with no rebuttal or second review cycle. The accepted papers will be published in conjunction with the ECCV 2022 proceedings. All the paper submissions will be handled by Microsoft CMT. Please submit your paper to our CVEU CMT Console [To be updated]. For detailed formatting instructions, and LaTeX templates please refer to the ECCV 2022 Author Instruction.



Invited Submission

The Invited Submission Track welcomes published papers on the topics summarized above. We will invite the accpeted papers to be presented during the workshop.

*Submission Guidelines*

The papers submitted to the Invited Submission Track must be published work (e.g., accepted by ECCV 2022) related to topics of the CVEU workshop. All the submitted papers will have the opportunities to be presented during the workshop.
